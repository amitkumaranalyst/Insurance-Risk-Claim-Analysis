# 📊 Insurance Risk & Claims Analysis Dashboard

## 🔍 Project Overview  
This project presents an interactive **Power BI dashboard** designed to analyze insurance risk and claim patterns. It helps in understanding customer behavior, claim distribution, and key risk factors that impact insurance companies.

---

## 🎯 Objectives  
- Analyze total insurance claims and policy distribution  
- Identify high-risk customer segments  
- Understand claim trends based on demographics and vehicle data  
- Provide insights for better decision-making in insurance pricing and risk management  

---

## 📁 Dataset Description  
The dataset includes information related to:  
- Policyholders (age, gender, marital status, education)  
- Vehicle details (car make, year, usage)  
- Claim data (claim amount, frequency)  
- Geographic segmentation (urban, rural, suburban zones)

---

## 📌 Key Metrics  
- **Total Policies:** 37,542  
- **Total Claim Amount:** $187.8M  
- **Average Claim Frequency:** 0.5  
- **Average Claim Amount:** $5.0K  

---

## 📊 Dashboard Insights  

### 🚗 Claim by Car Usage  
- Private vehicles contribute the majority of claims  
- Commercial usage shows comparatively lower claim value  

### 🏙️ Claim by Coverage Zone  
- Claims are evenly distributed across zones  
- Urban and highly urban areas show slightly higher claim values  

### 👨‍👩‍👧 Demographics Analysis  
- Male and Female policyholders are almost equal  
- Age group **36–55** has the highest claim contribution  

### 🎓 Education & Marital Status  
- Customers with **Bachelor’s degree** contribute the highest claim amount  
- Married individuals have higher claim values compared to others  

### 👶 Kids Driving Factor  
- Policyholders with **0 kids driving** contribute the highest claim amount  
- Risk slightly increases with more young drivers  

---

## 💡 Key Insights  
- Middle-aged customers are the most claim-active group  
- Private car usage is a major contributor to claims  
- Education and marital status influence claim behavior  
- Urbanization plays a role in claim distribution
